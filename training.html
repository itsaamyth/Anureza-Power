Anureza Power Internship Guide

If you’re reading this, chances are that internship season is ramping up. There’s a ton of advice out there on every aspect of applying, but it can be hard to know where to start. Well, good news: We’re here to help. While internships are all about discovering yourself and exploring your career options, you want to lay some groundwork to ensure you’re on the best track. As soon as possible, start doing some reflective brainstorming to figure out what type of internship will work best for you. Think about your hobbies and interests, major, and preferred work style so, we at Anureza Power are always ready for all those up-to the mark tasks and along with this we take care of our every employees and interns so that they are understanding, learning and building their skillset day to day. Moreover, we at mainly focus for the practical knowledge other then any educational or bookish knowledge.



What you will learn ?

Teamwork

In an internship like this, you learn to work as a team without focusing entirely on yourself. You also develop patience in situations when you disagree with another member(s) of the team.

Problem Solving Skills

What real-life problems have you solved till now other than to decide what to wear the next day and how to dodge the upcoming assignment deadline? An internship introduces you to real-life work problems and hence develops your problem-solving skills

Time Management

Earlier, you could just miss a class because you had some personal commitments. During an internship which is almost the beginning of your work life, you can’t mark your absence on a regular basis.Hence, it helps you learn to manage your time better by maintaining a balance between your work and personal life, without harming any of them.

Work Ethics

You won’t really learn about work ethics until you are in a tangible work environment. In college education, we are used to making excuses for late submissions, short attendance and what not! But it is only when we are introduced to the actual environment that we learn work ethics

Responsibility

Often missed out in the list of soft skills, being responsible is an integral skill required in the job arena. Your internship experience makes you more responsible and accountable for what decisions you make and how you execute what’s been allocated to you
